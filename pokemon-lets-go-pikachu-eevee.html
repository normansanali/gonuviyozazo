Pokemon Let's Go Pokemon Locations Map

August 22, 2024
blog

Most of the pokemon in Pokemon Let’s Go Pikachu & Eevee can be found in the wild. The Kanto region, in which the game takes place, is relatively large, and since there are around 140 pokemon roaming the highways, you’ll probably have at least some trouble filling up your Pokedex. Thankfully, Nintendo have published a map with marked spawn locations for all the critters in the game. You can see it below, along with a breakdown of all the pokemon locations in Pokemon Let’s Go.

Keep in mind that these are not exact locations – they only represent the areas in which a certain pokemon can spawn. They’re also not going to hang around all the time – you might visit a location which regularly spawns Pidgeys, only to find none of them there. There’s a certain randomness to the spawn rates, which means you’ll probably have to do a lot of foot work in order to capture all 150+ pokemon included in Pokemon Let’s Go & Pokemon Let’s Go Eevee.

A lot of pokemon appear in more places than one, but there are rare specimens that only spawn in very specific places. Based on the official map Nintendo released and our own experience with the game, we’ve prepared a list of all the roads (and locations) in the game, which towns they connect and which pokemon you can expect to find on them. The list excludes the pokemon which have specific capture requirements, like Mewtwo and Meltan. We’ve also prepared a handy map for all those who have trouble remembering town and location names (like we do). The road to a full Pokedex is long, but with these tools at your disposal, all you need is determination.
