Cecile Biography

Jamaica-born dancehall reggae artist, Cecile  Claudine Charlton, she is well known for her hit “Hot Like We”. She has released other hit tracks such as  “good”, “Bad Gyal”, “waiting” and “If  You Have My Love”.

Cecile Dancehall Reggae Singer

Cecile was born and raised in Porus, and Mile Gully, Jamaica, spending most of her childhood in Mandeville, where her grandfather had previously been mayor. Ce’ Cile discovered music through her father’s friend, Michael “Ibo” Cooper of the Third World reggae band, and released the first single, “Beat of Her Heart.”

She applied her own touch to the classical reggae and brought it to the dance hall. Steven Ventura, director of Celestial Sound Recordings, discovered her in 1995. There came a time when she worked as a studio manager and recorded several demo tapes. She has appeared on stage as a vocalist for various bands, as well as writing and producing songs for artists such as Jah Mason and Christi.

Rise To Stardom

Her first major hit was the single “Can You Do the Job” with Sean Paul in 2001. The track was later included on his album Dutty Rock, which sold over two million copies in the United States, and also won the Grammy Award. She registered with Delicious Vinyl Records in 2003, but she didn’t have the chance to release an album. Then, she released singles in collaboration with other artists.

Cecile Famous

Today Cécile is known internationally. Her albums sell well in Germany and Japan amongst others. She appears as a guest singer on singles from Sean Paul, Carl Henry, Mono & Nikitaman, DJ Fresh, Doctor P, and D-Flame. Her single “Gold Dust” with DJ Fresh peaked at number 24 on the UK Singles Chart, with a Shy FX re-edit that reached number 22 in the UK and number 39 on the Irish Singles Chart.

The sale of all versions of “Gold Dust” is combined together enabling it to have sold in excess of 400,000 copies, receiving a Gold certification by the BPI, despite not reaching the top twenty of the UK Singles Chart.

Cecile Children’s Book

Cecile partners with Barnes & Noble for Children’s book. Dancehall deejay Cecile says she was inspired by her young daughter and friend to write a children’s book. Titled Oceana, the story tells the tale of a Jamaican mermaid who hails from the parish of St. Thomas and loves reggae music. Cecile says it all started when she assisted her friend with a party.

Oceana by Cecile was written in 2018 and will be printed by Barnes & Noble, the world’s largest bookseller and a leading retailer of content, digital media and educational products.T-shirts swim caps, and swimsuits designed by her six-year-old she shares with singer Chris Martin are in the pipeline.
