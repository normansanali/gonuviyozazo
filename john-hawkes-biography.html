Who is John Hawkes?

John Hawkes is an American actor and musician. He is mainly known for his role in Winter’s Bone, Martha Marcy May Marlene, and The Sessions.

John used to be in a music band named Meat Joy.

John Hawkes: Age, Siblings, Parents, Ethnicity, Education

He was born John Marvin Perkins to Peter John and Patricia Jeanne on September 11, 1959. His birthplace is Alexandria, Minnesota, United States.

He belongs to the mixed ethnicity of Norwegian, Swedish, Irish, English, and Danish.

Concerning his educational background, he attended Jefferson High School, and later on, moved to Texas.

Then, he enrolled at the St. Cloud State University, Minnesota.

John Hawkes: Professional Life, Career

John Hawkes made his professional film debut in the year 1985 and starred as a light man in a film titled Future-Kill. He made his T.V debut in the year 1991 and appeared as Jimmy in a TV film titled Sweet Poison.

His other appeared movies are Scary Movie (1989) as Warren, Freaked (1993) as a cowboy, Playing God (1997) as Flick, Identity (2003) as Larry, S. Darko (2009) as Phil, Contagion (2011) as Roger, Everest (2015) as Doug, and more.

His recent roles were Jim and Duncan in Unloved (2018) and The Peanut Butter Falcon (2019) respectively.

TV Series

Besides appearing in several movies, John also contributed to TV series. He was seen as Mark in Wings (1994), Evan in Dangerous Minds (1996), Arnie in Fantasy Island (1998), Matthew in Monk (2008), Viggo in Too Old to Die Young (2019), and more.

His TV popularity relates to series like Deadwood (2004-2006) and Eastbound & Down (2009-2013). He was seen playing the characters of Sol Star and Dustin Powers respectively.

Awards and Nominations

John Hawkes was mainly popularized after appearing in a film titled Winter’s Bone as ‘Teardrop Dolly’ for which he won more than 6 awards including ‘Detroit Film Critics Society Award’, ‘Gotham Independent Film Award’, etc.

He was also nominated for more than ten different awards. Also, his role in the film titled The Sessions as ‘Mark O’Brien’ made him win two awards and was nominated for the other 12 awards.
